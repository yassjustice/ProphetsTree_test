First Generation
The Origin of Humanity & First Revelation
Era Context: The beginning of human existence on Earth. Adam and Hawwa were created in Paradise, given knowledge, and then sent to Earth to establish humanity. This marks the start of divine guidance through prophets.
Adam
آدم عليه السلام
First Prophet • First Human • Father of Humanity
Son of Adam and Hawwa. Known for righteousness and offering acceptable sacrifices to Allah. Murdered by his jealous brother Qabil, becoming the first martyr in human history. His story teaches about righteousness, jealousy, and divine justice.
Qabil
قابيل
The Transgressor • First Murderer
Son of Adam and Hawwa. Became jealous when his sacrifice was not accepted while his brother's was. Committed the first murder in human history by killing Habil. His story serves as a warning about jealousy, anger, and transgression.
'Isa
عيسى عليه السلام
Prophet & Messenger • The Messiah • Word of Allah
Mother: Maryam (Virgin Mary)
Birth: Miraculous virgin birth by Allah's command
Scripture: Injil (Gospel)
Miracles: Healing sick, raising dead, creating birds from clay
Status: Ascended alive to heaven, will return
Final prophet to the Children of Israel, born miraculously to Virgin Maryam without a father. Given the Gospel (Injil) and performed extraordinary miracles including healing the sick, raising the dead, and creating birds from clay. Rejected by most of his people, he was raised alive to heaven and will return before the Day of Judgment as a sign of the end times.

الحواريون
Al-Hawariyyun • The Pure Ones
The twelve chosen disciples of 'Isa who believed in his message and supported his mission. Known as "Al-Hawariyyun" (the pure white ones) for their sincere faith. They requested the miracle of the heavenly table (Ma'idah) and were among the few who truly accepted 'Isa's divine mission among the Israelites.

The Final Revelation
Muhammad ﷺ - Seal of Prophets & Universal Messenger
Era Context: Six centuries after 'Isa, in the Arabian Peninsula where the descendants of Isma'il had preserved elements of Ibrahim's monotheism, Allah sent the final messenger to all humanity. Muhammad ﷺ came with the Quran as the eternal miracle, completing and perfecting the divine message for all time and all peoples.

أمهات المؤمنين - The Mothers of the Believers
Khadijah
خديجة بنت خويلد
First Wife • First Believer • Mother of Most Children
Noble merchant woman, first wife and first believer. Supported Muhammad ﷺ through early revelations and difficulties. Mother of all his children except Ibrahim. Known as "Tahira" (Pure). Died before Hijra, remembered as the greatest support in establishing Islam.

'A'ishah
عائشة بنت أبي بكر
The Beloved • Scholar • Daughter of Abu Bakr
Daughter of Abu Bakr, youngest wife, most beloved after Khadijah. Exceptional scholar who transmitted over 2000 hadiths. Known for intelligence, wit, and deep knowledge of Islamic law. Teacher of early Muslim community, especially women's issues.
Hafsah
حفصة بنت عمر
Keeper of Quran • Daughter of 'Umar
Daughter of 'Umar ibn al-Khattab. Widow of martyr from Uhud. Known for her literacy and was entrusted with the master copy of the Quran. Strong personality, defender of women's rights, and active in preserving Islamic knowledge.
Zaynab bint Khuzaymah
زينب بنت خزيمة
Mother of the Poor
Known as "Umm al-Masakin" (Mother of the Poor) for her exceptional charity and care for the needy. Widow of martyr from Uhud. Though married to the Prophet ﷺ for only short time before her death, she left lasting legacy of compassion.
Umm Salamah
أم سلمة هند
The Wise Counselor
Hind bint Abi Umayyah, among earliest Muslims and emigrants to Abyssinia. Widow of Abu Salamah. Known for exceptional wisdom, intelligence, and counsel. The Prophet ﷺ often sought her advice on important matters, especially regarding women and family issues.
Zaynab bint Jahsh
زينب بنت جحش
Cousin of Prophet • The Generous
Cousin of Prophet ﷺ whose marriage established important social reforms regarding adoption. Known for her generosity, skill in handicrafts, and charitable works. Her marriage was announced directly in the Quran, giving her unique distinction.
Juwayriyah
جويرية بنت الحارث
Bringer of Peace
Daughter of chief of Banu Mustaliq tribe. Her marriage brought peace with her entire tribe and led to freeing of many captives. Known for her devotion to worship, spending long hours in prayer and remembrance of Allah.
Umm Habibah
أم حبيبة رملة
Symbol of Reconciliation
Ramlah, daughter of Abu Sufyan (former enemy of Islam). Early Muslim who emigrated to Abyssinia. Her marriage symbolized reconciliation and showed Islam's power to unite former enemies. Maintained dignity despite family opposition to Islam.
Safiyyah
صفية بنت حيي
From Noble Jewish Family
Daughter of Huyayy ibn Akhtab, chief of Jewish Banu Nadir tribe. Descendant of Prophet Harun (Aaron). Her marriage promoted unity between Jewish and Arab communities and demonstrated Islam's inclusive nature.
Maymunah
ميمونة بنت الحارث
The Last Wife • The Blessed
Last woman to marry the Prophet ﷺ. Sister-in-law of 'Abbas, the Prophet's uncle. Known for her piety, devotion, and the blessed nature of her marriage which was celebrated during Umrah. Lived long after Prophet's death, transmitting his teachings.
Sawdah
سودة بنت زمعة
Second Wife • Caretaker
Second wife, married after Khadijah's death. Elderly widow who helped care for the Prophet's daughters. Known for her sense of humor, loyalty, and motherly care for the household. Sacrificed her time with Prophet ﷺ to 'A'ishah out of love.

Cousin of Prophet ﷺ, first male to accept Islam as a child. Husband of Fatimah and father of Al-Hasan and Al-Husayn. Fourth Rightly-Guided Caliph. Known for his courage in battle, wisdom in judgment, and deep knowledge of Islam. Part of Ahl al-Bayt.
Al-Hasan
الحسن
Grandson • Leader of Youth of Paradise
Eldest grandson of Prophet ﷺ through Fatimah and 'Ali. Known for his resemblance to the Prophet ﷺ and his noble character. Briefly served as Caliph before abdicating to unite the Muslim community. Leader of the youth of Paradise.
Al-Husayn
الحسين
Grandson • Master of Youth of Paradise
Younger grandson of Prophet ﷺ through Fatimah and 'Ali. Known for his piety and principles. Refused to give allegiance to Yazid and was killed in Karbala. Master of the youth of Paradise alongside his brother Al-Hasan.
Abu Bakr as-Siddiq
أبو بكر الصديق
Best Friend • First Caliph • Father of 'A'ishah
Closest friend and companion of Prophet ﷺ. First adult male to accept Islam. Father of 'A'ishah. First Rightly-Guided Caliph who unified Arabia and began expansion. Known as "As-Siddiq" (The Truthful) for his unwavering faith.
'Umar ibn al-Khattab
عمر بن الخطاب
Al-Faruq • Second Caliph • Father of Hafsah
Second Rightly-Guided Caliph, father of Hafsah. Initially opposed Islam but became one of its strongest supporters after conversion. Known as "Al-Faruq" (The Criterion) for his ability to distinguish truth from falsehood. Expanded Islamic empire greatly.
'Uthman ibn 'Affan
عثمان بن عفان
Dhu'n-Nurayn • Third Caliph

People of 'Ad
قوم عاد
The Arrogant Builders
Powerful tribe in southern Arabia, contemporaries of Hud. Known for their massive construction projects and physical strength. Their arrogance led them to reject Hud's message and they were destroyed by devastating winds.
People of Thamud
قوم ثمود
The Stone Carvers
Skilled builders who carved elaborate homes from rock faces and mountains. Contemporaries of Salih, they demanded a miracle and received the she-camel, but their transgression led to their destruction by earthquake and blast.
The Abrahamic Era
Father of Prophetic Nations & Monotheistic Foundation
Era Context: The pivotal era of Ibrahim (Abraham), who established pure monotheism and became the father of prophetic nations. His descendants through both Isma'il and Ishaq would produce the majority of subsequent prophets. This era includes the building of the Ka'bah and the establishment of pilgrimage rites.
Lut
لوط عليه السلام
Prophet • Nephew of Ibrahim
Nephew and companion of Ibrahim. Sent to the people of Sodom and Gomorrah who were engaged in immoral practices. When they rejected his message and threatened him, the cities were destroyed by Allah. Lut and his righteous family were saved except his wife.
Family & Mission

Ibrahim
إبراهيم عليه السلام
Prophet & Messenger • Friend of Allah • Father of Prophets
Title: Khalilullah (Friend of Allah)
Father: Azar (Terah) - idol maker
Birthplace: Ur of the Chaldeans (Babylonia)
Called "Khalilullah" (Friend of Allah). Broke the idols of his people and survived being thrown into Nimrod's fire. Established pure monotheism and built the Ka'bah with Isma'il. Father of two prophetic lines through his sons. His legacy includes the pilgrimage rites and the foundation of monotheistic faith.
Nimrod
النمرود
Tyrant King • Opponent of Ibrahim
Arrogant king of Babylon who claimed divinity and opposed Ibrahim's monotheistic message. Ordered Ibrahim to be thrown into fire when he destroyed the idols. His tyranny and claim to godhood made him a symbol of ultimate arrogance against Allah.
Sarah
سارة
First Wife of Ibrahim
Relationship: Cousin and first wife of Ibrahim
Miracle: Gave birth to Ishaq in old age
First wife of Ibrahim and mother of Ishaq. Initially barren, she was blessed with a son in her old age as promised by Allah's messengers. Known for her faith, patience, and hospitality to divine guests. Ancestor of the Israelite prophetic line.
Legacy
Mother of Ishaq (Isaac)
Grandmother of Ya'qub (Jacob)
Ancestor of Israelite prophets
Hajar
هاجر
Second Wife of Ibrahim
Origin: Egyptian, given to Sarah as servant
Trial: Left in Makkah desert with baby Isma'il
Second wife of Ibrahim and mother of Isma'il. Left with her infant son in the barren valley of Makkah, she searched for water between Safa and Marwa hills. Allah caused the Zamzam well to spring forth. Her actions became part of Hajj pilgrimage rites. Ancestor of Arab prophetic line.
Legacy
Mother of Isma'il (Ishmael)
Her search became Sa'i ritual in Hajj
Ancestor of Arab tribes including Quraysh
Isma'il
إسماعيل عليه السلام
Prophet • Ancestor of Arabs • Builder of Ka'bah
Mother: Hajar (Hagar)
Great Trial: Willingly offered for sacrifice by Ibrahim
Achievement: Built Ka'bah with his father
Son of Ibrahim and Hajar. Survived the great trial of sacrifice, showing complete submission to Allah. Built the sacred Ka'bah with his father in Makkah. Became the ancestor of Arab tribes, particularly Quraysh (Prophet Muhammad's tribe). Known for his patience, obedience, and devotion.

Son of Ibrahim and Sarah. Miracle birth in their old age as promised by Allah's angels. Father of Ya'qub (Israel) and grandfather of the twelve tribes of Israel. Ancestor of the Israelite prophetic line including Musa, 'Isa, and many others.

The Israel Generation
Ya'qub & the Twelve Tribes Foundation
Era Context: Ya'qub (Jacob/Israel) and his twelve sons who became the founding fathers of the twelve tribes of Israel. This era includes the story of Yusuf's trials and rise to power in Egypt, which led to the Israelites' migration to Egypt.
Ya'qub
يعقوب عليه السلام
Prophet • Israel • Father of Twelve Tribes
Father: Ishaq (Isaac)
Title: Israel (given by Allah)
Wives: Raheel (Rachel), Lea (Leah), and others
Son of Ishaq, grandson of Ibrahim. Given the title "Israel" by Allah. Father of twelve sons who became the ancestors of the twelve tribes of Israel. Known for his patience during separation from his beloved son Yusuf and his eventual reunion with him in Egypt.
Raheel
راحيل
Beloved Wife of Ya'qub
Most beloved wife of Ya'qub. Mother of Yusuf and Binyamin. Her beauty and character made her Ya'qub's preferred wife. Died during the birth of Binyamin, leaving Ya'qub to care for their sons with special affection.
